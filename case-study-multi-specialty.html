Case Study: Regional Multi-Specialty Clinic

Transforming Clinical Context Gathering and Patient Care Coordination

Challenge

Presha Multi-Specialty Clinics, a growing network of 12 clinics across three states, was struggling with a fundamental challenge in modern healthcare: enabling providers to efficiently gather and understand the complete clinical context of each patient. With specialists across 8 different disciplines and patients often seeing multiple providers, clinicians were spending excessive time reviewing fragmented reports and records before appointments.

Key challenges included:

  • Providers spending 12-18 minutes per patient reviewing scattered clinical information across multiple systems
  • Critical contextual information often missed due to time constraints and information overload
  • Specialists lacking complete visibility into treatments prescribed by other providers
  • Patients repeating their medical history multiple times across different specialists
  • Delayed care decisions due to incomplete information at the point of care
  • Administrative staff spending 15+ hours weekly compiling reports for provider review

Solution

Klyrone implemented a comprehensive solution combining our Clinical Diagnosis Assistant and Healthcare Agents to transform how providers access, understand, and utilize patient context across specialties.

Intelligent Context Summarization

We deployed our AI system to analyze and synthesize patient information from all sources (EHR, lab systems, imaging reports, specialist notes) into concise, specialty-specific clinical summaries tailored to each provider's needs and the specific appointment purpose.

Contextual Relevance Engine

Our AI identifies and highlights the most relevant clinical information based on the patient's current complaints, the provider's specialty, and known clinical relationships between conditions, medications, and treatments.

Pre-Visit Preparation Agents

Autonomous agents gather and organize all relevant clinical information before appointments, identifying potential gaps in documentation and proactively requesting missing information from patients or other providers.

Cross-Specialty Care Coordination

Our system creates and maintains a unified care plan visible to all providers, automatically alerting relevant specialists when changes are made that might impact their treatment approaches.

Implementation Approach

1

Data Integration

We established secure connections to all clinical data sources, including the EHR, laboratory systems, imaging repositories, and specialty-specific applications.

2

Specialty-Specific Training

We trained our AI models on specialty-specific clinical workflows and information needs, ensuring relevance for each provider type.

3

Provider Workflow Analysis

We conducted detailed workflow analyses with providers from each specialty to understand their unique information needs and decision-making processes.

4

Phased Rollout

We implemented the solution in phases, starting with primary care and cardiology before expanding to all specialties, allowing for continuous refinement based on user feedback.

5

Continuous Optimization

We established an ongoing feedback loop with clinical users to continuously improve the relevance and usefulness of the contextual information provided.

Key Learnings

Specialty-Specific Context

Different specialties require different types of contextual information, making customization essential for adoption and clinical utility.

Cross-Specialty Communication

Improving visibility into care decisions across specialties leads to better coordination and reduces redundant or conflicting treatments.

AI-Assisted Clinical Reasoning

AI can identify subtle clinical relationships and patterns across specialties that might be missed in traditional siloed care approaches.
